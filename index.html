HAPPY GRADUATION Dr.Liu

Roots in the Cold
In silence, plants must brave the freeze,
While winds grow sharp and saplings seize.
Genes respond in shifting streams—
Some whisper fast, some dream in dreams.
Chloroplasts flinch, then swiftly act,
As nuclei draft a slower pact.
Mitochondria stay still, restrained,
While balance in the cell is strained.
Yet in the thaw, the dance rewinds,
New songs are sung, new ties that bind.
From cold to warm, life's code replays—
A tale of light through icy days.
